DialoGPT

DialoGPT is the model that our chatbot is based on. It is a transformer- based chatbot derived from GPT-2. In order to create a response, the model encodes the prompt into something called context . To generate a response, the model will decode the context into the model's response. The model is also tunable , this mean that the model can continue to be trained to suit more niche cases. 

The dataset

The dataset used to create the DialoGPT is a large collection of Reddit  exchanges spanning from 2005 to 2017. This data was then  filtered  by removing non-English sentence, and keeping only plain text . The text was further filtered by removing instances where a large majority of the text had been seen before. After this filtering, the data set had 150 million dialog instances. 

Parameters

DialoGPT comes in three different varieties : small, medium, and large. The small variety has 117 million parameters, the medium has 345 million, and the large has 762 million . Some of the hyperparameters  associated with this model are the RNG seed, the max sequence length, the batch size, the learning rate , and the size of accumulation steps.

Background: Transformer Models

Encoder-Based

These models rely on the encoder part of the original transformer and use no mask so the model can look at all the tokens/words in the attention heads (The future). They are pretrained by corrupting the input tokens in some way and trying to reconstruct the original sentence. 

Decoder-Based

These models rely on the decoder part of the original transformer and use an attention mask so that at each position, the model can only look at the tokens before the attention heads. Decoder-based models are trained on the classic language modeling task: guess the next token having read all the previous ones.

Seq2Seq Transformer

They use both the encoder and the decoder of the original transformer. Their most natural applications are translation, summarization, and question answering. The original transformer model is an example of such a model (only for translation).

Multimodal

Multimodal models incorporate information from multiple sources as they mix text inputs with other kinds (e.g. images). Some tasks they can do are Visual Question Answering, Image Captioning, Creating Images from Text.

DialoGPT Structure

Our DialoGPT model is only a decoder-based transformer architecture that leverages a stack of masked multi-head self-attention layers to train on massive web-text data.

Why not RNN nor LSTM?

As a team, we started experimenting with our project using RNN. However, the results were decidedly unsatisfying. Subsequently, we used the state-of-the-art transformer models instead, as they are better, faster, and achieve higher fidelity.

Data Preprocessing

Cleaning Data

  • The first step was to turn our dataset into a useable form by preprocessing the text files and loading them into Python.
  • Removing unnecessary metadata, files, features, and rows of missing values.

Connect Conversations

  • Linking together lines to form coherent conversations is essential in achieving a functional chatbot.
  • We had many files, and we needed to connect every pair of questions and answers into one array.

Normalizing

  • Converting from Unicode to ASCII. ASCII contains only English characters which it is better to train the models on to achieve better results.
  • Removing unnecessary characters. We used regular expressions to remove unrelated letters for better computer readability.

Trimming the  Infrequent Words

  • We remove unfrequent words to let the model focus on the most important tokens. We treat our threshold as a hyperparameter.  

Trimming the Very Long Sentences

  • Our first experiments included using RNNs and LSTMs. They cannot work very well with longer sentences without having an attention mechanism, so our decision was to trim the sentences that had more than 20 words.

Storing the Final Pairs

  • After all unsuitable pairs were removed, the remaining cleared pairs had to be stored so that they could be used later easily.

Evaluation

The model uses several metrics to determine the quality of the response. One such metric is the Bleu score . This score is meant to find how close the model gets to the expected output, while not over punishing it for using a similar word. For example, if the expected output is "I'm fine," the bot would still be rewarded for outputting "I'm good." In addition, to further improve the responses, annotators answered some questions about the quality of the responses, how engaging they are, and their fluentness.
